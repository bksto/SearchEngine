Download Photo Entertainment trade magazine Variety is honoring Dana Lamb '75, '82 (B.A., M.A. art), emeritus professor of art and coordinator of the College of the Arts' entertainment art/animation concentration, for his impact on animation as a course of study in higher education. Lamb will receive Variety's "Animation Educator of the Year" award Tuesday, March 10, at Siren Studios. Lamb, a former department chair, developed the College of the Arts' entertainment art/animation concentration and has spearheaded relationships with animation studios and entertainment companies throughout Southern California. "Dana's accomplishments as an educator and an artist have been invaluable to Cal State Fullerton," said Jim Taulli, interim dean of the University's College of the Arts. "He has created paths of opportunity and collaboration for countless students as both mentor and instructor. I am thrilled that he is being recognized for his efforts in educating generations of artists who have accomplished so much, thanks to their talent and his dedication." Lamb will receive the award during Variety's "10 Animators to Watch" event, celebrating the talents and contributions of animators on the big and small screens.
