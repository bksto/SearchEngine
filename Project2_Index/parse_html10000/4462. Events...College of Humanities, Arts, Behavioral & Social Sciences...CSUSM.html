Though raised by loving parents and supported by his four older siblings, he often feels like an outsider, and endures many painful episodes, some self-inflicted, in his struggle to understand his identity. Approved for Adoption poignantly traces one man’s interrogation of the definitions of ethnicity, culture, and the concept of “home.” Being a Man and Feminist Mini-Conference Thursday, March 12 | 8:30 am - 12:45 pm | USU Ballroom Being a Man and a Feminist acknowledges the need for engaged critical dialogue challenging racialized and gendered inequities in the classroom and on campus.
