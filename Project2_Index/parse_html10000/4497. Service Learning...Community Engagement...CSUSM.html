The Corporation for National and Community Service defines service learning as "a teaching and learning strategy that integrates meaningful community service with instruction and reflection to enrich the learning experience, teach civic responsibility, and strengthen communities." The two major components of service learning in an academic course are: The student's participation in a community service activity connected to course materials and The student's active reflection on the meaning of that service Reflection activities may range from classroom discussions to journaling to research papers connected to the service activities. If you need assistance with service learning, office hours are available Monday, Tuesday, and Thursday from 12pm - 2pm in Craven Hall room 6121.
