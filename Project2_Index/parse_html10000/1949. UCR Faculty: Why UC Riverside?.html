Our Campus UCR transforms lives. The university has a reputation as a place where knowledge-seekers can thrive, including students who are the first in their families to go to college and graduate students who are well into defining their life's work. UCR faculty provide the launching pad for another generation of leaders - people who will make a difference not just in the region, but in the world. At UCR, we offer our faculty: State-of-the art facilities, laboratories and offices. Proximity to Los Angeles, San Diego and Orange county's metropolitan areas at a much lower cost of living. Multitudes of support services. A location close to recreation areas in the mountains, the desert and the beach. The university exists as a living laboratory where scholars can delve deep into issues facing California and the world. From issues like clean air and water and safe and secure food supplies, to the development of groundbreaking technologies are all woven into the fabric of the UCR learning experience. UCR is a place where dedicated researchers get the support and resources they need to make great strides. Even with those lofty goals, UCR remains accessible to students seeking an excellent education from dedicated and respected faculty. It provides an affordable option to faculty members who want to continue their work in California, with close access to the cultural amenities of major metropolitan areas nearby. Our People Our 850 faculty members run the gamut from established professors in the prime of their research to up-and-coming academics who are bringing exciting new ideas to established fields of study. UCR's faculty are known throughout the world as experts in their fields of study. Seven current and former faculty members have been elected to the National Academy of Sciences, which honors distinguished scholars for their work to advance science and technology and for helping society as a whole. Guggenheim Fellowships, grants that help provide researchers with time - from six months to a year -- in which they can explore their chosen discipline in depth, have been awarded to more than 50 UCR faculty members. More than 100 UCR faculty members have been elected fellows of the American Association for the Advancement of Science, a recognition of how these faculty members have made notable contributions to the advancement of science and its role in society. Faculty Profile Norm Ellstrand and Tracy Kahn UCR is more than just a place where solitary researchers reach their goals of personal discovery and professional advancement. The university is home to an increasing number of husband-wife faculty members, some of whom work together in the same department, often within walking distance of one another's labs. Norman Ellstrand, a professor of genetics and director of the Biotechnology Impacts Center, and Tracy Kahn, a lecturer in the Department of Botany and Plant Sciences and curator of the UCR Citrus Variety Collection, have been married for 25 years. They met when he was an assistant professor and she was a graduate student. Now they are both established members of the department. "There are just three doors between us," said Kahn, a lecturer in the Department of Botany and Plant Sciences and the curator of the UCR Citrus Variety Collection. Ellstrand is a professor of genetics in the same department. "We feel very lucky. I know his grad students and he knows mine. We enjoy each other's company and give each other added perspective on things."
