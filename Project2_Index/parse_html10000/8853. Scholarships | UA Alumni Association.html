The UA Alumni Association awards academic scholarships to students through its chapters and clubs program and with the charitable support of donors, friends, and the Arizona community. Awarding scholarships is one of the most important things we do at the UA Alumni Association. In fact, in the last year alone, we awarded $1 million in scholarships to more than 450 students through our chapters and clubs program. Scholarships provide access to college for students who otherwise could not afford it, bring the best and brightest minds to the UA, and energize the economy through employment, internships, and research. Questions? Contact Neysha Aguilar, clubs and diversity specialist, at neysha.aguilar@al.arizona.edu or 520-626-9327. Learn More 'A' License Plate Students who receive scholarship dollars from the license-plate program must be Arizona residents. In addition, UA students who are awarded Alumni Association scholarships must meet specific criteria established by the official Alumni Association chapter or club awarding the scholarship. Scholarships are awarded in two categories: Merit-based — to attract and retain students of exceptional academic quality, and need-based — for students who demonstrate great financial need.
