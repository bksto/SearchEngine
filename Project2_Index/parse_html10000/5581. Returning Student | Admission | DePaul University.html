> Admission & Aid > Types of Admission > Returning Student Returning Student Admission Process Main Page Content Returning Student Applicants Former DePaul undergraduate students originally admitted into an undergraduate degree or baccalaureate program must complete a returning student application if either of these conditions exist:  1. Absence from DePaul for three or more consecutive quarters, excluding summer sessions.  2. Academic dismissal from DePaul. A dismissed student can be considered for returning admission after a period of two quarters, excluding summer sessions. Please note that a dismissed student with serious deficiency may be required to demonstrate acceptable academic achievement at another accredited college or university before returning admission is approved. Courses to be taken elsewhere must be approved by the College office and a grade of C or better must be earned in all such coursework.  A dis​missed student must apply to their previous college. Once admitted a student can apply for Inter-College Transfer.  Apply Apply for readmission online. After a decision is made, students may then contact an academic advisor.  Contact the Office of Undergraduate Admission for the selection of courses that are appropriate for returning admission consideration to your college. Former DePaul graduate students seeking returning student admission should contact the appropriate graduate admission office.
