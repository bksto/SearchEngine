NOTE: bMail is not encrypted and should not be used for sensitive communications.  Appropriate and prohibited uses of campus email  bConnected Privacy FAQ Be mindful of security -- do not use bConnected to store or transmit sensitive data. bConnected and Box can be used to store and transmit general and Protection Level 1 data, as defined in the Data Use Agreement. However, bConnected and Box are NOT suitable for storing and transmitting Protection Level 2 data, such as notice triggering data, human subject research data and other actively regulated data (defined in the campus Data Classification Standard).  Protecting the confidentiality and integrity of Berkeley campus data is everyone's business. Phishing Scams  The latest wave of phishing attacks are becoming more sophisticated. These attacks gain access to an account in an attempt to collect your financial information along with other personal information.  Question any message that asks you to log into a site to verify your credentials, even if it looks like the normal Berkeley authentication page. If you are in doubt, please forward the message to consult@berkeley.edu. Please remember, we NEVER ask you to verify your identity over email! Check out our cyber security awarenesss month tips at https://security.berkeley.edu/phishing. You will find tips along with downloadable fliers to share with others in your department. If you compromise your account by responding to a phish, your access to email will be turned off until the situation is resolved.
