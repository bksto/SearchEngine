Email Google Plus LinkedIn Twitter Facebook October 31, 2014 On October 24 and 25, 2014, more than 500 Harvard alumni, faculty, and friends gathered in Dallas for Your Harvard: Texas.  The weekend event, which also marked the centennials of the Harvard Club of Dallas and the Harvard Club of San Antonio, was part of Your Harvard, a series of events that will be held throughout The Harvard Campaign to bring together the global Harvard community. Watch highlights from this Texas-sized celebration: The festivities began on Friday evening with a gala dinner, musical performances, and remarks by Drew Gilpin Faust, president of Harvard University and Lincoln Professor of History, who shared her vision for the future of the University. President Faust describes the impact of the Harvard experience as she addresses the crowd in Dallas. #MyHarvard pic.twitter.com/BCvcMuGzpm — Harvard Alumni Assoc (@HarvardAlumni) October 25, 2014 The gala ended with a centennial celebration finale for the Harvard Club of Dallas and the Harvard Club of San Antonio. Watch Harvard community members wish the clubs a happy birthday: On Saturday, the celebration continued with interactive sessions featuring esteemed faculty members. Topics included "The Revolution of Energy," featuring Daniel G. Nocera, Patterson Rockwood Professor of Energy; "The New Metropolis," featuring Edward Glaeser, Fred and Eleanor Glimp Professor of Economics and director of the Rappaport Institute for Greater Boston at Harvard Kennedy School; Joyce Klein Rosenthal, assistant professor of urban planning at the Graduate School of Design; and Charles Waldheim, John E. Irving Professor of Landscape Architecture and chair of the Department of Landscape Architecture at the Graduate School of Design, and facilitated by Catherine Marchus Rose AB '92; "HarvardX and the Future of Scholarship," featuring David Malan AB '99, PhD '07, Gordon McKay Professor of the Practice of Computer Science at the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences; and "The Arts and Universal Lessons in Leadership," featuring Amy C. Edmondson AB '81, PhD '96, Novartis Professor of Leadership and Management at Harvard Business School, and Diane Paulus AB '88, the Tony Award–winning artistic director of the American Repertory Theater and professor of the practice of theatre, and facilitated by Maxwell L. Anderson PhD '81, The Eugene McDermott Director of the Dallas Museum of Art. Prof. @davidjmalan gives alumni in Dallas an inside look at Harvard's most popular undergraduate course #MyHarvard pic.twitter.com/F9dwxWDXZI — Harvard Alumni Assoc (@HarvardAlumni) October 25, 2014 The Saturday program also featured remarks by Richard W. Fisher AB '71 and concluded with panel discussions led by alumni experts in the fields of energy and health care. A sincere thank you to the many individuals and organizations that helped make this event possible. Your Harvard: Texas was hosted by the Harvard Alumni Association together with the: Harvard Club of Dallas Harvard Club of Dallas Centennial Committee Harvard University Club of Houston Harvard Club of San Antonio Harvard Club of Austin Read more about Your Harvard: Texas in the Harvard Gazette.  Your Harvard's next stops: Seattle, Beijing, and Chicago. Learn more about the Your Harvard event series.
