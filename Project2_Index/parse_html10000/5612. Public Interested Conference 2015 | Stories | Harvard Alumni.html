Public Interested is a Wintersession conference collaboratively planned by the Center for Public Interest Careers, Institute of Politics, Office for Sustainability, Phillips Brooks House Association, Harvard Alumni Association, Office of Career Services, and the Public Service Network, with coordination provided by the Assistant Dean of Harvard College for Public Service. During this day-long conference, students were invited to explore public interest careers through the lens of alumni experience and particular attention was focused on enhancing this alumni community.  Harvard Yard before the Public Interested Conference. Over 350 students and 150 alumni came to participate in events such as a keynote address from Senator Elizabeth Warren, the third annual Alumni Think Big: Big Public Service Ideas, and panel discussions, career advising, networking, and recruiting in nine public service career tracks, including human rights and public interest law, education and youth work, international development, and green careers. For a detailed schedule of the event, including a list of panelists and other speakers, please visit the 2015 Public Interested Conference website. The annual conference first took place in 2012, and has grown in popularity each year. The goal of this event is to provide undergraduates with opportunities to learn from alumni serving in a variety of public interest fields and to strengthen the alumni community for those working in and committed to the public interest sector. The Public Interested Conference was one of many events held this past January as part of Wintersession, the period of time between Winter Break and the beginning of the new semester which allows students to return to campus to participate in a variety of programs. To learn more about public service initiatives at Harvard, please visit the Public Service at Harvard College website.
