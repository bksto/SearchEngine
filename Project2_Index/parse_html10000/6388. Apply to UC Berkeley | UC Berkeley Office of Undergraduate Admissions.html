Admission is a two-step process: Admission Requirements and Selection. We accept the UC application. Requirements (Eligibility) Students must meet University of California requirements, detailed online at the University of California website. Becoming a Golden Bear requires more than a test score. To be Berkeley requires persistence. And passion. You stand up and you stand out. Selection UC Berkeley receives far more applications than it can admit, and to gain admission, you need to present an academic profile much stronger than that represented by the minimum UC admission requirements. To be as competitive as possible in the Berkeley applicant pool and to succeed within the holistic review, follow these steps: Read the admission requirements for UC Berkeley and what it takes to be competitive in our applicant pool. Freshmen Transfer students International students Read the requirements for the specific UC Berkeley college to which you are applying. If you are unsure of your intended major, learn more about our academic majors and minors. College of Chemistry College of Engineering College of Environmental Design College of Letters & Science College of Natural Resources Haas School of Business (for junior transfers only) Use all parts of the application. Be sure to make the most of each section of the application: the Personal Statement, Activities and Achievements, and Additional Comments boxes. Tell a complete story of your unique academic and personal achievements. For example, use the Additional Comments box to convey any information that will help us understand the context of your achievement, list additional honors/awards, or share information regarding a nontraditional school environment or unusual circumstances. Choosing a major is a personal choice; you may start in one direction and end up in another. While you may apply as an “Undeclared” or “Undeclared, Engineering,” we recommend that you apply in your major of choice if you know what you wish to study. If you are undecided, meet with an adviser. Or, you may design your own major! Majors & Minors Academic Support Dig deeper How do I find help once I am on campus?
