This Professional Certificate Program Provides: A well-rounded package of accounting courses for general accountants, or those who may desire to take the California CPA exam. Tools and principles in intermediate and advanced accounting as well as auditing, financial statement analysis, managerial accounting, income taxes (individual), and corporate taxation. Intended Audience:Accounting professionals working as accountants in public accounting, industry, or government, individuals seeking entry into these accounting careers, and individuals wishing to study specific subjects without pursuing the certificate.   Courses available in Santa Barbara   Courses available Ventura   Enroll in Certificate Program   Contact the Certificate Advisor   Information on Textbooks Requirements Candidates for the Certificate must successfully complete a total of 36 units of course work with grades of "C" or better. Most students take two to three years to complete the program; however, you may proceed at your own pace, depending on how many courses are available each quarter. Students have five years from the date their first course ends to complete the program. Course Prerequisites Students must have completed one semester or two quarters of course work in fundamentals of financial accounting prior to enrolling in Intermediate Accounting I, the entry course in the Certificate. UCSB Extension offers Principles of Financial Accounting I and Principles of Financial Accounting II to fulfill this prerequisite. Similar courses can also be taken at community colleges or at four year institutions. Students are responsible for meeting individual course prerequisites prior to enrolling in courses. Prerequisites ensure that all students have the appropriate background before entering a course. Recommended Course Sequence It is suggested that students who have completed the prerequisite coursework in fundamentals of financial accounting begin concurrently taking the three-quarter Intermediate Accounting I, Intermediate Accounting II, and Intermediate Accounting III sequence. Intermediate Accounting I  is offered each fall and spring. Specific courses are open to those who do not wish to complete the entire program as long as any individual course prerequisites are met. Course Credit As the continuing education division of the University of California at Santa Barbara, UCSB Extension is fully accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC). All courses in the program are approved for professional-level, post-graduate credit. This type of credit is typically recognized by government and industry for full or partial tuition funding or reimbursement. Course units and/or continuing education units (CEU) values are listed with the course descriptions. Completion of the program shows evidence to a prospective or current employer of recent professional-level training and development in this field for career advancement and/or salary increase purposes. Questions concerning transferability of credit toward degree programs at other schools should be directed to the degree-granting institution. Transfer Credit Professional Accounting: Transfer credit is limited to 8 quarter units. Proposed transfer courses taken at other schools must be comparable in content and length to the UCSB Extension equivalent and must have been taken from an accredited college or university within the last five years. To determine whether courses taken at other schools will qualify, send a copy of the course title and description and the dates the course was taken along with the UCSB Extension course title equivalent(s) to the certificate advisor. The advisor will inform students whether the coursework is transferable. To activate the formal transfer of credit, students must have filed their Application for Candidacy in the certificate program and must request the school previously attended to send an official transcript to the certificate advisor. Applying for Candidacy To be accepted as a candidate in a certificate program, students must first submit the Application for Candidacy Form below and a one-time, nonrefundable filing fee of $100. Students will be expected to fulfill program requirements in place at the time candidacy is filed. Students will automatically be notified of acceptance once the application and filing fee are received. Allow four to six weeks for applications to be processed. Acceptance letters are mailed upon acceptance into the program. Certificate candidates must notify the UCSB Extension certificate advisor by phoning (805) 893-3816 or by e-mailing when they receive the grade for their final course. Once your final grade is received and your records are checked to verify that you have met all program requirements, your certificate is processed and mailed to you. Duplicate certificates are available if requested in writing. There is a $22 charge for each duplicate certificate. Course Formats/Locations Courses typically meet evenings, weekly, for 10 to 12 sessions with occasional Saturday classes. The program is available in both Santa Barbara and Ventura Counties. Some students accelerate their progress by commuting between the Santa Barbara and Ventura locations. Instructors The instructors who teach in this program are practicing professionals from the community. They are chosen for their personal commitment, academic background, teaching ability, and professional expertise. We provide students with the opportunity to comment on our instructors, our program, and our customer service at the end of each class. We welcome any comments that would assist us in improving the quality of instruction in our program.
