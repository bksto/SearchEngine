From nanotechnology to international studies, the campus is home to a variety of departments conducting outstanding research in a wide range of disciplines. Over half of all graduating seniors collaborate with faculty members on original research or creative projects. For a complete list of campus centers conducting ongoing research projects, visit the UC Santa Barbara Office of Research.   Fast Facts In 2014 UCSB was ranked as the No. 2 public university in the world by the Leiden Ranking for its impact on the sciences. The campus is home to more than 100 research units, including 11 national centers funded by federal agencies and departments such as the National Science Foundation. As one of only 62 institutions elected to the Association of American Universities—a list that includes Harvard, Yale and Rutgers, to name a few—UCSB stands among the top 1.5 percent of all universities in the U.S. and Canada.
