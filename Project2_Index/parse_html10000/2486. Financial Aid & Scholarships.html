FAFSA - Apply for Most Aid With One Application Blue and Gold Opportunity Plan Attend UCR for Free The Blue and Gold Opportunity Plan covers (all!) tuition and student services fees for qualified California residents whose families earn less than $80,000 a year. If you already have partial aid, Blue and Gold can help make up the difference. Some students can even get help with books, housing and transportation. Learn more about the Blue and Gold plan and see if you qualify. Scholarships Earn a Scholarship Scholarships abound! They can be awarded for everything from special talents and leadership ability to showing promise in your future career. UCR offers many. There’s a high chance you will find one right for you. Search UCR scholarships and fellowships. Discover scholarships from outside agencies. Pay in Installments Deferred Payment Plan Pay your tuition and fees in monthly installments with our Deferred Payment Plan (DPP). Another great way to relieve financial stress and focus on what matters – like your finals! Contact Student Business Services for details. Grants Get a Grant Grants are funds you don’t have to pay back! By submitting a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA), you are automatically applying for many grants including UCR grants, Federal Pell Grants, Cal Grants (with a verified GPA form) and more. Research grants offered by departments or outside agencies for additional free funding. Learn more about FAFSA. Learn how to apply for a Cal Grant. Learn more about federal grants. Work at UCR Work-Study Select from on-campus jobs, earn a paycheck, gain experience and establish a schedule that works well with your studies. In 2013-2014, hourly rates at UCR ranged between $8-$15. If you don't qualify, you can still apply for great on- and off-campus jobs through our Career Center. Learn more about Work Study. Student Loans Low Interest Rates When selected carefully, student loans can offer low interest rates and ample time to pay them back. Loans can come from the federal government, UCR, private lenders and more. Learn more about student loans. Applying for financial aid is easier than you think! You don’t have to be overwhelmed. By completing the Free Application for Federal student Aid (FAFSA), you are automatically applying to most sources of available aid, including the Blue and Gold Opportunity Plan and Cal Grants (with a verified GPA form).  Fill out a FAFSA even if you don’t think you will qualify. UCR has aid available for students at all income levels. You have nothing to lose, and everything to gain – including a free (or close to free) education! Learn how to file your FAFSA.
