View a complete list of past recipients   2014 Alumni Award Recipients Distinguished Alumnus Award Michael Devirian ’66 The most prestigious of the honors bestowed by the UCR Alumni Association. The award is based on national and international distinction in one's field and significant contribution to humankind. Michael Devirian ’66 received his degree in physics. While at UCR, Michael was President of the Student Body and Managing Editor of The Highlander. He has had a long and distinguished career managing space missions at the Jet Propulsion Lab (JPL) in Pasadena and at NASA headquarters in Washington, D.C. Immediately after graduating from UCR, Michael went to work for JPL as a mission controller of the ongoing space missions. He has many career highlights while working at JPL and NASA which include working as Mission Control for the Mars Mariner and Lunar Lander Missions from 1966-1970. He was the operations manager for the Hubble Space Telescope and Camera Installation. He also worked on the global data systems for Earth resource management through the United Nations Environmental Program. Alumni Service Award William Kroonen ’60 Honors superior service in the public sector or a sustained pattern of volunteer service in the community, arts or for the benefit of UCR that has positively represented the University and fellow citizens. Bill Kroonen ’60 received his degree in Spanish and went on to earn his masters from University of New Mexico and his Ph.D. from the University of Southern California. He had a distinguished career in education. He served on the Desert Sands Unified School District’s Board of Education from 1981-1994, including four terms as president. In 1994, he was elected to the Riverside County Board of Education, a position he held until 2010. Throughout his life he has been engaged in public service. He has given countless hours as a volunteer including service on a number of community boards and committees over the years, including the McCallum Theatre Board, the Regional Access Project Foundation Board and the Board of the Riverside County Fair and National Date Festival. He was named the Palm Desert Rotary Club’s Citizen of the Year in 2013. Bill was recently appointed as the only community representative on the University Planning and Development Committee that coordinates educational programming and facility use of UCR-Palm Desert Center and CSUSB-Palm Desert for Coachella Valley. Outstanding Young Alumnus Award Shah Selbe ’04 Presented to alumni under the age of 35 with a significant record of career and/or civic achievement and promise in their profession. Shah Selbe ’04 received his degree in chemical engineering. He currently works as a spacecraft propulsion scientist working on liquid and ion propulsion systems for satellites and other unmanned spacecraft for a commercial satellite program. Outside of his professional life, Shah is a conservation technologist and technology expert working to identify and implement innovative approaches to ocean conservation. In this capacity, he has worked with a number of influential nonprofits and government organizations worldwide including; Center for Ocean Solutions, Monterey Bay Aquarium, New England Aquarium and the National Geographic Society. Most recently his work resulted in National Geographic naming him as one of their 2013 Emerging Explorers and was granted a $10,000 award for research and exploration.
