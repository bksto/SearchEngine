DePaul Faculty and Staff Additional ways you can make your gift to DePaul: Automatic Payroll Deduction Sign up online for automatic payroll deductions (requires a Campus Connection username and password). If you are already signed up for an automatic payroll deduction and would like to make changes to it, please contact Gift Processing at (312) 362-8666 or giving@depaul.edu. Online Use our secure online form to make a credit card gift. Make a Gift Now You can also make an ongoing gift from your credit card account by using the button above and choosing "Multiple/Recurring Gift" under the gift type section. Please choose from the following options: Mail Make your check payable to DePaul University, and send your gift to: DePaul University 1 East Jackson Blvd. Chicago, IL 60604-2287 Attn: Advancement Gift Processing Once your gift has been received and processed, a receipt for tax purposes will be mailed to you. Online Use our secure online form to make a credit card gift or pledge. Make a Gift Now Automatic Funds Transfers From Your Checking or Savings Account To sign up for automatic funds transfers from your account, please download a hardcopy request form and mail it to the address at the top of the form or call Advancement Gift Processing at 312-362-8666. Automatic Credit Card Charges To sign up for automatic recurring donations from your credit card account, please go to our main donation page and choose “Recurring Gift” under the Contribution Type section. Alternatively, you can download a hardcopy request form and mail in your request or call Advancement Gift Processing at (312)362-8666. Stock Transfers To obtain instructions for transferring shares of stock, click here. For more information on making electronic funds, stock transfers or recurring credit card charges, please contact Gift Processing at (312) 362-8666 or giving@depaul.edu. Planned Gifts To learn more about planned giving, please contact one of our planned giving professionals. To make a planned gift, fill out the estate gift confirmation form.
