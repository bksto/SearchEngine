Every College of Education program sets their own deadlines and requires the submission of additional materials. These materials vary by program, but examples include state certification, samples of your work, and personal statements. Information about required materials is available from the graduate admissions advisor  or faculty lead of each program area. Graduate Application Requirements (Domestic and International) Complete a online graduate application for admissions. Domestic Application Fee: $45.00 USD International Application Fee: $75.00 USD A submission of required documents. Doctoral Applicants - Only An independently completed writing sample composed for a professional or scholarly audience. Submit the writing sample electronically in the graduate application, under the listing “Additional Documents”.  Doctoral applicant’s program department will notify eligible candidates for an interview. Admission Deadlines (Domestic and International) The college reviews completed applications at the program admission deadline.The College's Office of Graduate Studies recommends applicants to acquire their official and supplemental documentation (e.g. test scores) prior to applying for admissions.   Application Decision Each program area has a faculty committee who reviews completed applications.  The program area faculty considers your credentials and educational background before making the final decision on admissibility.  The decision period varies by each program area.  The College's Office of Graduate Studies recommends applicants to check their online application for status updates.
