BEST June 11 - August 8, 2014 Building Excellent Scientists for Tomorrow (BEST) is a summer program developed by Bradley University’s Center for Stem Education and designed for high school students interested in careers in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math. Participants are placed in research labs in the Peoria area for 150-300 hours of research experience, working side-by-side with top researchers and their teams to learn first-hand about the research process. Students will also receive additional training in science methods, scientific ethics, safety and related topics.
