Application Deadlines How to Apply Portfolio Requirements Portfolio Content (per program) ﻿Application Deadlines The College of Architecture’s graduate admissions deadlines by program are the following: Master of Architecture Level I & II: February 1 (Fall admission only) Master of Science in Architecture: February 1 (Fall admission only) Master of Arts in Architectural Studies: February 1 (Fall admission only) Master of Science in Industrial Design: October 1 (for Spring admission and consideration of fellowships); February 1 (for Fall admission) ﻿How to Apply Candidates interested in attending the College of Architecture should begin by applying online here. In addition to the application documents required on the Graduate School website, applicants to the College of Architecture must also submit the following: $50.00 application fee payable to the University of Houston. This may be paid online. Please send the College of Architecture an additional copy of the official transcript showing undergraduate degree conferral. The mailing address is:                        University of Houston                        College of Architecture                        Graduate Admissions                        122 College of Architecture Building                        Houston, TX 77204-4000 Portfolio ﻿Portfolio Requirements Purpose The portfolio allows reviewers to determine an applicant’s potential for success in a studio based design curriculum. Evidence of creative work is not limited to Architecture, Interior Architecture, or Industrial Design work. Examples of creative work may include designs, drawings, photographs, paintings, sculpture, writing, or other creative pursuits. Please include a brief explanation of work submitted. Portfolio Requirements DO NOT send the portfolio to the Graduate School or Office of Admissions. Please send directly to:                        University of Houston                        College of Architecture                        Graduate Admissions                        122 College of Architecture Building                        Houston, TX 77204-4000 Portfolios should not be larger than 8 1/2" x 11" or A4 format. All work must be reproductions submitted in a single, bound volume. Oversized portfolios, flat work, rolled drawings, slides, or CD/DVDs will not be accepted. Any loose work will not be reviewed. Do no submit original documents. Identification The front of the portfolio must be clearly labeled with the following information: The applicant’s name and e-mail address. The degree program to which the applicant is applying (i.e. Master of Architecture Level I or Level II, Master of Science in Architecture, Master of Science in Industrial Design, or Master of Arts in Architectural Studies). Organization The organization of the portfolio is a design exercise. It should be clear, concise, and thoughtfully composed to best reflect the applicant’s interests and skills. The quality of the work is of greater importance than the quantity. Portfolio Return Portfolios will not be returned unless specifically requested, and a suitably sized, self-addressed and pre-stamped envelope has been provided. ﻿﻿Portfolio Content Master of Architecture—Level I The portfolio is not expected to show architectural skills. Rather, it should contain work that demonstrates facility with a variety of media and illustrates three-dimensional understanding. Applicants may demonstrate their creative, artistic, and perceptual skills through painting, sculpture, or other three-dimensional explorations. Photography as an example of creative work must demonstrate intention in composition, color/light, or other critical points of view. Master of Architecture—Level II, Master of Science in Architecture, Master of Science in Industrial Design, and Master of Arts in Architectural Studies Applicants should submit examples of work demonstrating an ability to pursue study at an advanced level. The portfolio should be of high quality and provide evidence of well-developed graphic skills, a sense of design, and an understanding of space, form, and volume at different scales. The review committee assumes the portfolio represents the applicant’s best abilities in design.
