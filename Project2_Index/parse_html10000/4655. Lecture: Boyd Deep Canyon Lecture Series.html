Is this anniversary a cause for celebration or despair? What have we learned during the past four decades? Why is endangered species conservation so complicated? This talk will address these questions, place them in a broader historical context, and discuss some of the challenges and opportunities for biodiversity conservation in the twenty-first century. Peter S. Alagona is an associate professor of history and environmental studies at the University of California, Santa Barbara. RSPV HERE Parking is free.
