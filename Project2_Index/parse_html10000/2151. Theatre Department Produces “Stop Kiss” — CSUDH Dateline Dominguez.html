News Releases / Theatre Department Produces “Stop Kiss” Theatre Department Produces “Stop Kiss” February 17, 2015 by Amy Bentley-Smith Leave a Comment The California State University, Dominguez Hills (CSUDH) Department of Theatre and Dance presents its third show of the 2014-15 season — the funny, sensitive love story “Stop Kiss.” Show dates and times are Feb. 27 and 28, March 6, 7, 13 and 14 at 8 p.m., and March 1, 8 and 15 at 2 p.m. in the Edison Theatre. **Due to the strong adult language and adult themes in the play, “Stop Kiss” is not recommended for young children. ** Written by American playwright Diana Son, “Stop Kiss” is the story of Callie and Sara, who meet out of necessity when Sara needs someone to care for her cat. They find that they are attracted to one another first as friends and then as something deeper. Their first kiss provokes a violent hate crime attack; the ensuing events change their lives and redefine their relationship. “‘Stop Kiss’ is a beautiful and funny play about falling in love,” said theatre department lecturer Kelly Herman, who is directing the CSUDH production. “This play has interwoven in it the joy of new love with the complexities of people’s prejudices and fear, of themselves and of others. It explores commitment, friendship and human compassion. Love is unexpected and to be treasured whenever and with whomever; it is a gift.” Originally staged off-Broadway in 1998 to critical acclaim, “Stop Kiss” was hailed by Charles Isherwood of Variety as “a poignant, funny play about the ways both sudden and slow that lives can change irrevocably,” and by the New York Times as “irresistibly exciting… a sweet, sad and enchantingly sincere play.” Following the March 1 and March 6 performances, the director and cast will host a talkback discussion of the play with members of the lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, questioning and ally (LGBTQA) community. “Stop Kiss” will be performed in the intimate 70-seat Edison Studio Theatre, part of the University Theatre Complex. Tickets are $15 for general admission and $12 for students and seniors. Group rates are available. For more ticket information or to purchase tickets online, visit: csudh.edu/theatre/tickets.htm. For other payment options, call the box office at (310) 243-3589. CSU Dominguez Hills is located at 1000 E. Victoria St., in Carson. The University Theatre complex off Toro Center Drive on the east side of campus. For a campus map, visit: www.csudh.edu/VisitUs/campusmap.shtml. Parking in campus lots is $5, payable using cash, debit or credit cards at kiosks in each lot. For more information about “Stop Kiss” or the CSU Dominguez Hills Theatre and Dance program, contact (310) 243-3588 or visit http://www4.csudh.edu/theatre-arts.
