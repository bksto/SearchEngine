September, 1951 Classes convened on the new campus in temporary buildings. The students move in wherever possible. The parking lot was situated on the side of a hill, and every time it rained, the vehicles slid down the hill, into each other in the mud. Click on thumbnails to view large versions of photos: Dr. P. Peterson and the future campus layout. This is the library when the campus first moved to its new home. Students spend a profitable hour in the reserve reading room. November 16 Inauguration of the permanent campus site was acknowledged at a "campus warming" ceremony. New & Improved Student Facilities New student services were established or improved from the prior years to accommodate the students such as the library, a health office, The 49er Canteen, lounge, bookstore, and placement office. Spring, 1952 Enrollment continued to increase up to 2,000 students with more than 80 instructional staff.
