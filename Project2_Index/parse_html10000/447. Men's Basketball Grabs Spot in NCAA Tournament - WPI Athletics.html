Knight Tennis Courts & Throwing Area Donahue Rowing Center Alumni Poly Club Hall of Fame Office of Development and Alumni Relations WPI.EDU Close Men's Basketball Home Schedule and Results Roster Statistics News Coaching Staff Archives 1,000 Point Scorers Men's Basketball Grabs Spot in NCAA Tournament Mar 02, 2015 Worcester, MA --- For the ninth time in the last 11 years, the WPI men's basketball program will be participating in the NCAA Division III Men's Basketball Tournament.  The Engineers nabbed a coveted Pool C bid into the 62-team tournament and begin play Friday in a first round contest against host St. John Fisher at 7:30pm. WPI is 21-5 on the season and ranked 12th in the most recent D3hoops.com Top 25.   The Engineers finished second in the competitive NEWMAC, which received three bids to the tournament, before falling to Springfield Saturday in the conference tournament semifinals. The 2015 tournament will mark the tenth time WPI has reached the NCAA Tournament, with nine coming in the last 11 seasons under head coach Chris Bartley.  Ticket prices are $6 for adults and $3 for surents and seniors.  Children under 12 are admitted for free. The WPI-St. John Fisher winner will take on the Amherst-Sage winner Saturday evening at 7pm.   As mentioned, the 2015 tournament will feature three NEWMAC teams as conference tournament champion Babson with host Westfield State and at-large selection Springfield with take on WPI's 2014 opponent Albertus Magnus. The announcement caps an impressive six days of NCAA selections for WPI.  Last Wednesday it was learned that Men's Swimming and Diving will be sending the its first relay (800 yard freestyle) in school history to the NCAA Championships later this month.  This past weekend, women's diver Veronica Nikolaki (Athens, Greece) participated in the NCAA Regional at Springfield and on Sunday wrestler Brian Amato (Newington, CT) posted a pair of victories at his regional to qualify for the NCAA Championships in two weeks in Hershey, PA.  Additionally, Laura Pumphrey (East Hampton, CT) enters the final week before Track and Field selections as one of the top high jumpers in the country.
