International Student Life: Student Clubs, Organizations, Initiatives A list of clubs and opportunities hosted by the International Student Resource Center. English language, Certificate and Postgraduate Diploma programs Many different short-term English language, Certificate and Postgraduate Diploma programs are offered by UCR’s International Education Programs, Extension. International Education Programs also develops Custom Programs for groups. Host Family Programs Some international students who attend UC Riverside choose to live in the home of an American family during their stay. The host families provide a room, three meals a day and transportation to UCR for classes and UCR sponsored activities. This experience is a great opportunity to immerse yourself in American culture. International Women's Exchange The International Women’s Exchange meets every Wednesday morning from 10 a.m. to 12 p.m. in the International Student Resource Center. The program allows women of all nationalities to come together exchange culture and ideas and to build friendships. It is a great opportunity to make new friends and learn more about a variety of cultures. Resources for Faculty and Other Scholars Visa Assistance The International Scholar Center processes process visas for international faculty and scholars. The three main visa types utilized by the ISC to gain lawful status for faculty and scholars are the J, H-1B and O visa types. Academic Personnel for Academic Appointments The Office of Academic Personnel facilitates the recruitment, development, and retention of academic employees at UC Riverside. Teaching Abroad Opportunity - Education Abroad Program, Education Abroad Program - Term, Education Abroad Program - Year Information on submitting proposals to teach courses abroad in the Education Abroad Program. Teaching Abroad Opportunity - Summer Teaching Information on submitting proposals to teach courses abroad in the Summer Travel Study Program. Academic Senate Committee on International Education A part of the UCR Academic Senate, the committee represents the division in all maters concerned with international education, including international research, and study-abroad and foreign exchange programs. International Collaborations A Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) is a written statement of understanding between UCR and a foreign university or non-profit institution expressing a mutual intention to engage in a cooperative academic or research effort which does not involve a commitment of university resources.
